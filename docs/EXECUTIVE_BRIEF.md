# Executive decision brief

## The decision

The 2031 Census is expected to be increasingly digital-first. The practical question is not simply which local authorities had the lowest online response in 2021, but where a digital-first operating model could create the greatest risk of exclusion—and which places provide the most useful portfolio for testing interventions.

## Recommendation

Use a five-location research and intervention portfolio from the group report:

| Location | Portfolio role |
| --- | --- |
| Boston | High intensity across all fixed schemes and strong sensitivity robustness |
| Blaenau Gwent | High intensity across all fixed schemes; distinct deprivation context |
| Kingston upon Hull | Population-scale lens; prevents the portfolio from favouring only smaller areas |
| Conwy | Highest reported weighting robustness and consistent fixed-scheme risk |
| North Norfolk | Strong weighting robustness; ageing and rural-access context |

This is a purposeful coverage portfolio, not a claim that these five authorities are the only places requiring support.

## What the evidence says

- The OLS model across 328 local authorities produces a paper-first coefficient of **−0.546** and **R² of 0.964**. Raw online completion is therefore strongly entangled with the 2021 contact strategy.
- Six authorities appear in the top 12 under all three fixed schemes: **Boston, Blaenau Gwent, Denbighshire, Conwy, Carmarthenshire and Ceredigion**.
- The reported 1,000-draw sensitivity test shows that the precise order is weight-dependent. Conwy has the highest reported top-12 frequency at **76.8%**.
- A ranking alone is insufficient: intensity, affected-population scale and underperformance capture different operational risks.

## Priority actions

1. Test first-contact designs first, including automatic paper or assisted-digital fallbacks after non-engagement.
2. Separate **mode need** from **contact need** so poor digital access and poor contactability are not treated as the same problem.
3. Use trusted local organisations and messengers in prototype testing.
4. Define an equity outcome such as the gap in non-response between identified at-risk groups and the overall population.
5. Combine area prioritisation with qualitative research; never infer an individual's needs from an area score.

## Evidence boundary

The regression, residuals, fixed-scheme ranking intersection and figures have been independently reproduced from the surviving 328-authority reference output. The original raw age, deprivation and English-proficiency columns were not preserved, so the exact reported six-component scores and Monte Carlo frequencies cannot yet be regenerated end to end. The recommendation is suitable for exploratory prioritisation, not operational deployment without refreshed source data and stakeholder validation.
